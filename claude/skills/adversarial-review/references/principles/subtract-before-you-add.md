# Subtract Before You Add

**Principle:** When evolving a system, remove complexity first, then build. Deletion creates a simpler substrate that makes subsequent additions cleaner, smaller, and less error-prone.

## Why

Adding to a complex system compounds complexity. Removing first reduces the surface area, reveals the essential structure, and makes the addition's design more obvious.

## The Pattern

- **Sequence removal before construction.** In plans, schedule deletion phases before build phases.
- **Cut before you polish.** Cut ruthlessly to the minimum before investing in quality. Half-finished features are worse than missing ones.
- **Design for observed usage, not speculative edge cases.**
- **Simplify prompts.** Remove redundant instructions, excessive templates. One-line instructions often work as well as 15-line templates.
- **When a reference has no novel content, delete it** rather than leaving a stub.
